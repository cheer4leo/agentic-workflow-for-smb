What we found from public information

Desert Air Plumbing & HVAC is a local, family-owned plumbing and HVAC company serving the Phoenix metro area. The company offers 24/7 emergency service, online scheduling, free estimates, and same-day appointments. The website promotes call, text, and form-based intake channels.

Based on the service mix, 24/7 availability, and multiple intake channels, the company likely experiences high call volume, after-hours inquiries, and unbooked estimates that compete for limited office staff time.

Identified Opportunities

Revenue-critical tasks that fall through the cracks

These are common lead response and follow-up gaps seen in HVAC and plumbing companies with a similar profile.

Missed-call recovery

Calls that go to voicemail during peak hours, after hours, or when techs are on-site. Each missed call is a potential job that may go to a competitor if there's no fast response.

High impactRevenue recovery

Quote request follow-up

Customers who requested an estimate via the website or phone but never booked. Without consistent follow-up, these warm leads go cold within 24-48 hours.

High impactConversion

After-hours lead capture

Late-night and weekend inquiries from the website, voicemail, or text that sit unprocessed until Monday morning. Fast acknowledgment keeps the customer engaged.

Medium impactSchedule fill

Maintenance plan & repeat service nurture

Past customers who haven't been contacted about seasonal maintenance, equipment check-ups, or annual service plans. Recurring revenue left on the table.

Steady valueRetention

Post-job review & referral requests

Completed jobs where no review or referral was requested. A timely follow-up drives Google reviews and word-of-mouth without extra office effort.

Steady valueGrowth

Recommended Pilot Workflow

Start here: Missed-call text-back & quote follow-up

We recommend starting with missed-call recovery because it has immediate revenue impact, clear triggers, and a simple workflow that doesn't require system integrations to start.

Missed-Call & Quote Follow-Up WorkflowPilot design
1

Detect missed call or new form submission

Call tracking or website form logs a missed call, voicemail, or quote request. Agent is notified of the new lead.

⚡ Agent-assisted
2

Send fast text-back acknowledgment

Within minutes, a pre-approved text goes out: "Hi, this is Desert Air. We saw we missed your call — how can we help?" Uses owner-approved templates only.

⚡ Agent-assisted
3

Qualify and categorize the lead

Agent logs the service type, urgency, and preferred contact method. Emergency requests are flagged immediately for dispatch.

⚡ Agent-assisted
4

Route to office or dispatcher

Qualified leads are routed to the right person with a summary. Office handles scheduling, pricing, and customer conversations.

👤 Staff action
5

Follow up on unbooked estimates

If a quoted lead doesn't book within 24-48 hours, agent drafts a follow-up for staff review: "Still need help with that AC repair?"

⚡ Agent-assisted
6

Track and report lead outcomes

Agent logs lead status (booked, pending, lost, no response) in a simple sheet. Weekly summary shows response time, conversion rate, and missed opportunities.
