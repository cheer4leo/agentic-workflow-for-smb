Workflow Audit: Schedule Recovery Opportunities

An assessment of recurring follow-up tasks that may benefit from AI-assisted workflows with practice-approved messaging and staff oversight.

Practice Snapshot

What we found from public information

Sunshine Family Dental is a 2-provider general and cosmetic dentistry practice serving the central Phoenix metro area. The practice offers hygiene, restorative, cosmetic, and emergency services with online scheduling and same-day emergency appointments. They have 340+ reviews across Google and Yelp.

Based on the service mix, team size, and public signals, the practice likely has recurring follow-up work that competes with same-day patient demands for front-desk time.

Identified Opportunities

Tasks that create revenue if they happen consistently

These are common schedule-recovery tasks seen in practices with a similar profile. Each one is too small for a full-time hire but too valuable to ignore.

Overdue hygiene recall

Patients who completed their last cleaning 7+ months ago without scheduling the next visit. Most practices have 30-60% of their hygiene base overdue at any given time.

High impactRevenue recovery

Unscheduled treatment plans

Diagnosed restorative or cosmetic work where the patient left without booking. These cases often represent $500-$5,000+ per patient and only need a well-timed, approved follow-up.

High impactHigh-value cases

Cancellation gap filling

Same-day and next-day cancellations that leave open chair time. Matching gaps with ready-to-book patients fills schedule holes that would otherwise be lost production.

Medium impactSchedule fill

Post-visit review & referral requests

Patients who completed treatment but were not asked for a review or referral. A timely follow-up builds the review count and generates referrals while satisfaction is highest.

Steady valueGrowth

Insurance claim follow-up

Submitted claims that need status checks, resubmissions, or patient balance communications. Tedious, recurring work that directly affects collections.

Medium impactCollections

Recommended Pilot Workflow

Start here: Overdue hygiene recall follow-up

We recommend starting with hygiene recall because it has the highest volume, the lowest risk, and the most predictable workflow.

Hygiene Recall WorkflowPilot design
1

Export overdue patient list

Office staff exports an approved list of patients overdue for hygiene from the practice management system.

👤 Staff action
2

Prioritize and segment

Agent scores patients by months overdue, treatment history, and contact recency. Segments into tiers: 7-12 months, 12-18 months, 18+ months.

⚡ Agent-assisted
3

Draft follow-up messages

Agent creates personalized, practice-approved outreach for each tier using templates reviewed by the office.

⚡ Agent-assisted
4

Staff reviews and approves batch

Office manager or front desk reviews drafted messages. Edits, removes, or approves each one before sending.

👤 Staff approval
5

Send and track responses

Approved messages are sent via the practice's existing channels. Agent tracks responses and flags interested patients.

⚡ Agent-assisted
6

Surface ready-to-book replies

Interested replies are queued for the front desk to schedule. The office handles all booking directly.

👤 Staff books
